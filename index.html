About Us

Professional Nanny Service LLC is an organization that prepares experienced, qualified childcare professionals for parents. We thoroughly research the background of every caregiver — conducting advance interviews and verifying details such as whether they have ever held any criminal liability.

We comply with the ISO 9001:2015 quality management system standard. We operate a domestic-service worker training center accredited by the Vocational Education and Training Center (MBSYT) under the Ministry of Labor and Social Protection.

Professional Nanny Service trains its caregivers into qualified, professional childcare providers at its own training center.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a nanny do during the day?+

While caring for the child, the employee shall provide knowledge related to the child's age-specific and psychological characteristics, upbringing, and care and nursing. At all times, the employee has a duty to read books and magazines that promote the child's development, and to work in a manner suited to the child's age. The employee has a duty to dress the child according to the weather and physical condition, to take the child outside regularly for fresh air, and to air the child out in a location that ensures the child's safety while playing. If any abnormal behavior or unfavorable health condition is observed in the child, the employee shall immediately notify the lady of the house. If this occurs during a long absence of the parents (such as traveling to the countryside, going abroad, etc.), the employee has a duty to notify them by phone or other means and obtain instructions and advice, to administer medicine with permission, and to take the child to the doctor and hospital designated by the mother, respectively. The childcare worker has a duty to constantly sanitize and clean the child's clothing, toys, other belongings, and household items such as cups and spoons, and has a duty to carry out wet cleaning and ventilation daily in the area around the child in households with an infant, and 2-3 times a week in households with a child over 1 year of age. The childcare worker also performs tasks such as preparing healthy, good-quality, tasty food suitable for the child.

What training do the caregivers complete?+

All caregivers are trained at our own accredited training center, which operates under the Ministry of Labor and Social Protection (МБСҮТ). Training covers communication and attitude, personal development; cleaning and service; crime prevention; child growth, development, and child protection; childcare and nursing; food and nutrition preparation. And most importantly every caregivers will pass a official examination before we recommend them to you.

What is a nanny's work schedule like?+

We offer three schedules: Live-in (6 days a week with 1 day off), 5 Working Days (5 days a week during working hours with 2 days off), and Selected Days (1–4 fixed days a week during working hours).

What kinds of nannies are available?+

We will introduce you to people aged 18-45 who have undergone health examinations, had their personal information verified, and completed training, and you will be able to make your own selection.
